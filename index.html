Q1

How can the appropriate use of free time improve your physical and mental well-being?

🌿
🔵 Stative Verbs

What We Know and Feel

Free time feels essential, not optional. Research shows that the brain needs regular breaks to consolidate memory and regulate emotions. Most people understand this in theory, yet they believe that stopping is a form of laziness. It isn't. Rest seems passive, but internally, the body and mind remain very active during recovery. We know that chronic stress damages the cardiovascular system — free time is one of the few tools that genuinely counters it.

🏃
🟡 Action Verbs

What Free Time Does for Your Body

When people exercise or walk during free time, cortisol levels drop significantly. Sleeping enough strengthens the immune system. Practicing yoga or stretching reduces muscular tension. Students who read for pleasure build vocabulary and improve focus. Playing with friends or family releases endorphins. Even simply sitting in a park and breathing deeply lowers blood pressure. The body records every rest you give it.

💡
🔷 Modal Verbs

What We Can and Should Do

Free time can improve sleep quality when used intentionally. You should schedule at least 30 minutes of personal activity each day — not as a reward, but as a habit. Hobbies may reduce the risk of depression over time. Physical activity must not be confused with productivity; both matter. Anyone could start with something small: a walk, a song, a sketch. According to Cadavid-Ruiz et al. (2023), students who can balance study with leisure report higher perceived well-being.

Q2

What is the importance of relaxation activities in your life?

🧘
🔵 Stative Verbs

What Relaxation Means to Us

Deep down, everyone wants to feel at peace. Relaxation seems like a luxury, but it is actually a biological necessity. The brain belongs to a body that needs recovery. I believe that a person who knows how to rest understands something others miss: that output depends on input, and rest is the most undervalued input of all. Relaxation feels like a reset — and it genuinely is one, neurologically speaking.

🌸
🟠 Action Verbs

What Relaxation Actually Does

Meditating for 10 minutes a day lowers anxiety markers in the brain. Listening to music activates the parasympathetic nervous system, which slows the heart rate. Spending time in nature restores mental clarity. Drawing, writing, or cooking for pleasure redirects the mind away from stressors. According to Minsalud (2023), Colombians who practice regular self-care habits report fewer episodes of chronic stress.

⭐
🔷 Modal Verbs

What Relaxation Can Give You

Relaxation activities can prevent burnout before it starts — and this should matter to every student and professional. You must not wait until you break down to start resting. Yoga, breathing exercises, or even a walk in a park could reduce cortisol by up to 12% in a single session. Mindfulness practices may change the brain structure over time, improving memory and emotional regulation. You can begin with just ten minutes. That's enough.

📊 Colombian Data: According to the Encuesta de Salud Mental del Ministerio de Salud y Protección Social, 66.3% of Colombians have faced mental health challenges at some point in their lives (Minsalud, 2023). Among university students in Cali, those who regularly engaged in leisure activities showed lower levels of depression, anxiety, and stress (Cadavid-Ruiz et al., 2023). The data is clear: relaxation is not a waste of time. It's the work that makes all other work possible.
